Feed Quality and Durability: How to Optimize Your Feeding Program

Problem

Feed deterioration, crumbling pellets and oxidation reduce nutrient intake and animal performance.

Why it happens

Poor pellet integrity and oxidation degrade feed quality, especially in humid and hot storage conditions.

What to check first

Pellet durability, storage environment, freshness, and antioxidant protection.

Feed Quality and Durability: How to Optimize Your Feeding Program

The healthy growth and high performance of animals are directly linked to the quality of the feed used in their feeding programs. Feed quality is not just about nutritional value; it also includes physical durability, freshness, and shelf life. Durable feeds make the feeding process more efficient and reduce waste.

Feed quality has a direct impact on the health and productivity of animals. High-quality feed improves digestion, strengthens the immune system, and ensures animals benefit fully from nutrients. Low-quality feed can lead to digestive disorders, weakened immunity, and reduced growth.

Feed durability depends on technologies and ingredients used during production. Pellet binders maintain feed integrity and prevent dust formation, reducing feed losses and improving consumption efficiency.

Synthetic antioxidants preserve freshness and prevent oxidative deterioration, extending shelf life and maintaining nutritional value. This ensures animals continuously consume high-quality feed and reduces waste.

Fresh feed is essential for performance, especially in hot and humid environments where spoilage occurs quickly. Antioxidant protection stabilizes feed and improves animal resilience.

Practical Checklist

  • Regularly analyze feed composition
  • Improve pellet durability using binders
  • Protect feed with antioxidants
  • Improve storage conditions

Nutritional Approach

Improving feed quality requires both physical stabilization and oxidative protection. Combining pellet binders with antioxidant solutions ensures durability, maintains nutritional value and supports consistent animal performance.
